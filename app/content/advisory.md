## Where I'm useful

I take on a small number of serious engagements at a time. The pattern that
works: I'm the architect-on-call for a leadership team building AI capability
that has to actually ship, not just demo.

### For enterprises

- **Architecture review** of an in-flight AI program: what's structurally
  sound, what's going to break in the second year, what to stop building now.
- **Fractional CTO / Chief AI Architect** for a 6 to 12 month transformation,
  embedded with the executive team.
- **Workshops** for leadership teams: how to think about model choice, build vs.
  buy, governance, hiring, and the unglamorous adoption layer.
- **Due diligence** for acquirers and investors on AI-claiming targets.

### For founders (early-stage)

I keep a small mentorship cohort (typically 3 to 5 founders at a time).
This is unpaid for founders pre-Series A and is intentionally selective.

- AI product architecture and model strategy
- Technical hiring for the first 5 to 25 engineers
- Avoiding the standard scaling mistakes I've made at every prior stage
- Investor-facing technical narrative

## How I work

- **Written before spoken.** I'd rather read a one-pager than sit through a
  forty-minute deck. Whatever we discuss, expect me to write back.
- **Deep over wide.** I do my best work when I'm engaged seriously with a few
  teams, not casually with many. I won't take on an engagement I can't be
  useful inside.
- **Decisions, not status.** Engagements produce decisions and architecture
  artifacts, not slide decks of observations.
- **I write under my own name.** Anything I produce for you stays with you.
  Anything I publish publicly, whether essays, frameworks or talks, is mine
  and on this site.

## Who I work with best

- Leadership teams that have already decided to build AI seriously and want
  someone who's been through the second-year problems.
- Founders building AI-native products who want an architect, not a coach.
- Boards and investors who need a second opinion on AI-heavy investments.

## Who I'm probably not the right fit for

- "AI strategy" engagements that don't intend to ship anything.
- Hourly consulting at scale.
- Engagements where I can't be in the room with the people doing the work.
